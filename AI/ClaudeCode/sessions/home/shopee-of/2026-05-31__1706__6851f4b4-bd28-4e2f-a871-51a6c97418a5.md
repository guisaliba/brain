**Does the outbox pattern solve the commit problem? Partly — and the part it *doesn't* solve is the important one.**

The transactional outbox solves *dual-write*: write business state + an "I owe this external action" row in one transaction, then a relay performs the action **at-least-once** with consumer-side dedupe. It guarantees you never *lose* an intended side-effect.

But the Shopee commit isn't a dual-write problem.
